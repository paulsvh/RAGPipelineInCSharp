# Italian Pasta Dishes

Classic Italian pasta relies on simplicity, quality ingredients, and precise technique. The following recipes represent foundational dishes every home cook should master.

## Spaghetti Carbonara

**Servings:** 4 | **Cook time:** 20 minutes | **Difficulty:** Moderate

### Ingredients
- 400g spaghetti
- 200g guanciale (cured pork cheek), cut into small lardons
- 4 large egg yolks plus 2 whole eggs
- 100g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
- Freshly cracked black pepper

### Instructions
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente, about 8-10 minutes. Reserve 250ml of pasta water before draining.
2. While the pasta cooks, render the guanciale in a cold skillet over medium heat. Do not add oil. Cook for 6-8 minutes until the fat is translucent and the edges are crisp.
3. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, whole eggs, and most of the Pecorino, leaving some for garnish. Add generous black pepper.
4. Remove the skillet from heat. Add the drained pasta to the guanciale and toss. Wait 60 seconds for the pan to cool slightly, then pour in the egg mixture, tossing vigorously. Add pasta water a splash at a time until the sauce is glossy and coats each strand.
5. Serve immediately, topped with remaining Pecorino and black pepper.

**Common mistakes:** Adding the egg mixture to a pan that is too hot will scramble the eggs. The residual heat should gently cook the sauce into a creamy emulsion, not a chunky omelette. Never use cream — authentic carbonara gets its richness entirely from eggs and cheese.

## Cacio e Pepe

**Servings:** 4 | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Difficulty:** Moderate

### Ingredients
- 400g tonnarelli or spaghetti
- 200g Pecorino Romano, very finely grated (use a Microplane)
- 2 tablespoons whole black peppercorns, coarsely cracked

### Instructions
1. Toast the cracked peppercorns in a dry skillet over medium heat for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add a ladle of pasta cooking water and let it reduce by half.
2. Cook the pasta in well-salted boiling water until just shy of al dente. Reserve 500ml of starchy pasta water.
3. Transfer the pasta to the pepper skillet with a ladle of pasta water. Toss over medium-low heat.
4. Remove from heat and gradually add the Pecorino in small handfuls, tossing constantly and adding splashes of pasta water to maintain a smooth, emulsified sauce.

**Common mistakes:** Adding all the cheese at once causes it to clump into a solid mass. The key is patience — add cheese gradually while tossing, and keep the temperature moderate. Use starchy pasta water (cook the pasta in less water than usual to increase starch concentration).

## Bolognese (Ragu alla Bolognese)

**Servings:** 6-8 | **Cook time:** 3-4 hours | **Difficulty:** Easy (but slow)

### Ingredients
- 300g ground beef
- 200g ground pork
- 100g pancetta, finely diced (a brunoise cut works well here — see the knife skills guide)
- 1 large onion, finely diced
- 2 carrots, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced (this onion-carrot-celery combination is a classic mirepoix)
- 150ml dry white wine
- 400ml whole milk
- 400g crushed San Marzano tomatoes
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- Salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg
- Fresh tagliatelle or pappardelle to serve

### Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a heavy Dutch oven. Cook the pancetta until its fat renders, about 4 minutes. Add the mirepoix vegetables and cook until softened, about 8 minutes.
2. Add the ground beef and pork, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Cook until browned.
3. Pour in the wine and stir, scraping up fond from the bottom. Let the wine cook off completely.
4. Add the milk and nutmeg. Simmer gently until the milk has been mostly absorbed, about 15 minutes.
5. Stir in the tomatoes and tomato paste. Bring to a bare simmer — you want occasional lazy bubbles, not a rolling boil. Partially cover and cook for 3-4 hours, stirring occasionally. Add water if the sauce gets too thick.
6. Season with salt and pepper. Serve over fresh tagliatelle.

**Common mistakes:** Rushing the cook time. A proper Bolognese needs hours of slow simmering to develop depth. The milk step is essential — it tenderizes the meat and adds a subtle sweetness. Skipping it yields a harsher, less complex sauce.

## Aglio e Olio

**Servings:** 4 | **Cook time:** 15 minutes | **Difficulty:** Easy

### Ingredients
- 400g spaghetti
- 100ml extra-virgin olive oil
- 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
- Fresh parsley, roughly chopped
- Salt to taste

### Instructions
1. Cook the spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 250ml pasta water.
2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-low heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes. Cook gently for 3-4 minutes until the garlic is golden — not brown.
3. Add a ladle of pasta water to the skillet (it will sizzle). Toss in the drained pasta and parsley. Toss vigorously for 1-2 minutes, adding more pasta water as needed to create a light, glossy sauce.

**Common mistakes:** Burning the garlic. Garlic goes from golden to bitter in seconds. Keep the heat low and watch it closely. The dish depends on a handful of ingredients, so each one must be treated with care. Use the best olive oil you can find — it is the primary flavor.
